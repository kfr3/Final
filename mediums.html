Colored Pencils were introduced to the public through art companies Faber-Castell and Caran d'Ache during the early 1900's. Most colored pencils today have a graphite and clay core, but higher quality colored pencils typically have a waxy core, which gives off a higher pigment and is easier to blend. However, most people point to Joseph Nicephore Niepce's camera made in 1825 as the first camera that was able to capture and save the picture that it took. Digital

The most modern medium to this list, digital devices as a medium to create art is a much more recent occurance compared to the others on this list. Digital Art emerged during the 1980s, as computer engineers experimented with different painting programs on computers. These painting programs developed into more detailed applications, such as Photoshop and Clip Studio Paint, which are able to mimic the look of real art materials like paint brush strokes and the grit of pencils.
