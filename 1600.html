Hokusai

❮ ❯

Katsushika Hokusai was born on October 31st, 1760, and is one of the most notable Japanese artists that specialized in ukiyo-e. Ukiyo-e is a type of printmaking art style that was created during the late 1600s, and began a popular style during the 18th century. Hokusai claimed his individuality in this genre by having bold and flat designs compared to his peers, and focusing on nature and common features of life rather than beauty and fanatsy, as other ukiyo-e artists tended to focus on.
