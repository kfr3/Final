Banksy

❮ ❯

Banksy is thought to born sometime during the 1970s in Bristol, England, and their career as a street artist started during the 1990s. After being in a street graffiti group, similarly to Jean-Michel Basquiat, Banksy began to debut exhibitions throughout England, which caused an interest in Banksy as an artist as people could not predict where he would debut his art or what it would be about. Banksy tends to make art concerning current events, morals, and inequality in the world, as their piece Cardinal Sin was a response to child abuse being revealed in the Catholic Church. Njideka Akunyili Crosby

❮ ❯

Njideka Akunyili Crosby was born in 1983 was raised in Engugu, Nigeria. After moving to the United States to study, she realized her love for art during her final year of school at Swarthmore College, and began to pursue it rather than the medical field she was planning to get into. Crosby's unique style is derived from photos that she uses from Nigerian magazines as well as her own photos, and layers them in a process that invovles other mediums such as paint, colored pencil, and fabric. In this process, she is able to convey her experience as a Nigerian-American woman to the world, and she felt that there was a lack of representation of intersecting cultural identities.
